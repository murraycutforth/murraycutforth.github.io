*11th February 2022, Lost Valley Buttress*

In January I managed to switch my work down to a four day week. I really enjoy my job most of the time but there's probably nothing which I want to spend 40 hours, 5 days a week doing year-round. I see it getting more and more common in my peer group and I really hope it catches on in this post-single-earner-per-household world. It was pretty handy this winter as it felt like there was often decent conditions for mixed climbing on the Friday before everything would deteriorate a bit on the weekend. On one such Friday me, Will and Rob headed up to Lost Valley Buttress for a look at Neanderthal.

Will and I have some good history with the route, after we bailed off in the teeth of a huge storm a few years ago. Back then Will basically led us up the first two pitches, and then we both took one look at the big third pitch buried in rime and immediately abseiled off!

This time the experience was about as different as it could possibly be. No wind, minimal rime, and three on the rope made it all very chilled. Rob led the first pitch which is pretty easy but has some interesting thin ice moves getting into the belay cave. Pitch 2 was weird. On the one hand it was my return to proper climbing after a big house moving/christmas/covid/crap conditions break, but having climbed the pitch before really took the edge off. Although the second half in the corner had no resemblance to the 10m of unprotected snow climbing which Will led in storm Dennis, this time it was proper mixed climbing on hooks and plastered on snow blobs. Good to feel really in tune with the climbing again.

Will took the lead on the big pitch 3 after an emotional reunion with our pink tat left of the belay a couple years earlier. One of most ludicrously fun pitches out there in my book. It looks way harder than it really is, which usually creates brilliant climbing as you're constantly happily suprised by good gear and hooks. The top was a pretty unique sequence with a big pull through a roof/overlap, on to an ice bulge with a few moves of pure ice to pull over.
