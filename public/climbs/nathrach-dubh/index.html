Nathrach Dubh

VI, 6 Carn Etchachan, Cairngorms with Tim 26 February 2023

26th February 2023, Carn Etchachan

Some photos from one of the best days of the winter, when Tim and I headed over to Carn Etchachan and climbed Nathrach Dubh (aka. Black Snake). Walking over the tops was pretty special as we watched cloud pour into the Loch Avon basin.

I would say that I’ve become a bit of a Carn Etchachan devotee over the past couple of winters, even though I’ve only scratched the surface of the routes there. In the gorgeous weather, Tim and I spent ages scoping out almost every route in the first two amphitheatres as we traversed in. Since unrepeated 1* grade VIs are in vogue these days, someone should go and have a look at Pythagoras (https://www.ukclimbing.com/logbook/crags/ben_macdui_-_carn_etchachan-14648/pythagoras-61007). From where we were standing, it looked wildly unlikely at that grade!

Pythagoras takes the obvious crack and then traverses left onto a ledge on the skyline..!

We climbed Nathrach Dubh in four short-ish pitches. It’s a bit indistinct at the start, but from the second pitch onwards it’s a single direct corner/crack system which slowly steepens and becomes more and more sustained, and feels like the epitome of traditional mixed with hand jams, knees, torques, and all kinds of thrutching required.

The final two pitches featured some really thought provoking moves. Tim found an incredibly tenuous pick-to-head jam in a flared pod which seemed to be the only way to make progress at one point.. And the final exit from the face up a hanging groove was an amazing sequence of laybacking/back and footing.

All that remained was to wander out in the sunset.
