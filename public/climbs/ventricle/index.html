VII, 8 Coire an Lochain, Cairngorms with Rob 13 February 2022

13th February 2022, Number 1 Buttress, Coire an Lochan

Rob and I wandered in to lochan for an easy day, and stumbled out well after dark after ventricle. A post of few words and many pictures:

Great view of most of the route from number 2 buttress. I’m just getting stuck into the crux move of pitch 2, where you rock up onto a big flat foothold and stretch miles over left for a mediocre hook which lets you lower yourself into the corner. Then you get a breather on a ledge before a wild overhanging groove up to the belay. Pitch 1 was too desperate for any pictures! Conditions low down were just the way we like it- marginally acceptable..

The top half of the route, me and Rob on the belay ledge below P3. The route goes up a big corner above, and then the scary looking offwidth, before a final pitch of easy ground.

Yarding my way up the amazing P3 corner. Trying to second quickly I got in a flap when the hooks on the left wall turned out to be rippy bottomless seams and enjoyed cutting loose onto my top tool a few times before getting back in the corner.. A good lead from Rob.

Rob pulling over the final wide crack! We were pretty psyched to be past this point. I finished leading ths pitch just as darkness fell. I spent much longer than I needed to on this section really, it looked horrific but actually was pretty amenable with minimal offwidthing required. Long arms/axes to hook the chockstone from as far aways as possible are very helpful.

Rob at work on the top out pitch
